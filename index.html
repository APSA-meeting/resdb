What Is It?

The research residency wiki database is an exciting application that provides detailed information on residencies focused on research. This system provides the critical material needed for not only students to make an informed decision about their next big career step, but also allows programs an opportunity to recruit the best students for its curriculum. However, just like the actual wikipedia itself, this project depends on help from volunteers like you to make it happen. Please see below for the simple things you can do to help out.

How to Contribute

Join the technology committee

The technology committee consists of the individuals that build and maintain all of APSA's critical technology infrastructure, including the research residency wiki database. Joining the technology committee is an exciting opportunity to contribute to APSA and add something to your CV. Although the committee has technology in the name, being a programmer is not necessary for helping out with the wiki database as information acquisition and entry into the wiki does not require any programming experience. If you are interested, please contact:

Tim Kennell Jr., Technology Committee Chair

Donate your program's info

As much of the detailed information on research residency programs is not readily available online, this initiative depends on the donations of information directly from the program itself. If you are a program director, faculty member, resident, or fellow that would like to contribute your's programs information, please contact:

Residency Research Wiki Database Team

Contact your program director

If you are a trainee at the institution or in the program and would like your program's information to be contributed but are not sure how to proceed, the best place to start is contacting your program director to make them aware of this opportunity. By contacting your program director, you not only make them aware of this initiative, but also might help bring programs to our attention that we can add to the database in order to include as robust a list of research residency programs for students and programs alike.
